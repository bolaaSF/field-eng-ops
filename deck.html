What's Broken

Five structural problems compound into unpredictable field operations

Each problem makes the next one worse. They need to be solved thoughtfully and sequentially.

Revenue Management Is Lagging

The team currently influences revenue but does not manage it. Managing means forecasting, providing intel to reps, and ensuring opportunities are visible to maximize usage across the organization.

No Cross-Team Visibility

SE activity invisible to Developer Success. No unified customer timeline. No handoff protocols. Context drops at every transition.

Cannot Track Consumption

Comp transition to consumption is in motion but no infrastructure to measure it. Product feedback loop broken: win/loss, support patterns, competitive objections all siloed.

CRM Creates Burden, Not Leverage

No automated capture. Deal recordings and call transcripts exist but are not being leveraged to populate CRM, health scores, or handoff context.

Shared Technical Services Is New with Significant Opportunity

STS can be the unlock that creates visibility across all teams. The core design of this function will be crucial. Done right, it becomes the connective tissue between pre-sale, post-sale, and expansion.

Revenue Intelligence

Consumption tracking as the revenue lens

The shift to consumption economics requires account-level usage visibility that translates into revenue per rep, not just aggregate platform metrics. This applies across enterprise, self-serve, and startup segments.

Enterprise Accounts

Track consumption at the account level, tie it to the rep, and surface expansion signals alongside stalled adoption. This is how the team moves from influencing revenue to managing it: forecasting, providing intel, and ensuring opportunities are visible.

Self-Serve and Startups

These accounts are often invisible to Field Engineering today. Consumption tracking identifies which self-serve accounts are growing into enterprise candidates and which are hitting usage ceilings that require attention.

Churn Signals from Usage Costs

When consumption costs outpace the value a customer perceives, that is a churn signal. The system must detect when usage patterns suggest a customer needs optimization guidance or contract restructuring before they leave.

Contract Optimization

Not every account needs more consumption. Some need restructured contracts that align cost to their actual usage patterns. This is a retention play, not just an expansion play. Proactive restructuring builds trust and prevents surprise billing churn.

Cross-Functional Cohesion

How Field Engineering builds cohesion across functions

Field Engineering does not operate in isolation. The operating system must strengthen how Sales, Product, and executive leadership work together, not just how Field Engineering works internally.

With Sales

Shared pipeline visibility
  • Technical validation and deal velocity become a shared motion, not a handoff
  • Expansion signals are surfaced jointly, not passed over a wall
  • SE activity connects directly to pipeline so both teams see the same picture

With Product

Structured intelligence loop
  • Win/loss, competitive signals, and feature gaps flow into Product with revenue context
  • Friction patterns surface across accounts, not as isolated anecdotes
  • Product and Field Engineering develop a shared language around what customers need

With Executive Leadership

Measurable enterprise motion
  • Field Engineering's influence on revenue becomes visible, not assumed
  • Consumption data creates a shared foundation for board-level reporting
  • Operating rhythm connects functions instead of creating silos with meetings
Compensation

Consumption comp model

Three phases. Never change comp on data ICs do not trust.

Phase 1

Shadow Metrics

Track consumption alongside current comp. No changes. Build IC trust in the data.

Phase 2

Blended

60% committed / 40% consumption growth. Add shared team metrics.

Phase 3

Consumption-Forward

Only after 2+ quarters of clean data. Full shift to consumption-based compensation.

Prerequisite

Define the unit of expansion first. Deployments? Bandwidth? v0 credits? Teams? Every comp model collapses if built on the wrong unit.

Execution

30-60-90 day ramp

Listen first. Design with the teams. Then build.

Days 1–30

Listen, Map, Build Trust

  • 1:1s with every team lead and Sales Operations partnership
  • Audit data infrastructure, map the actual customer journey across all five teams
  • Follow 10 accounts end-to-end
  • Quick win: deploy deal recording to CRM automation
  • Early artifact: deliver first unified account timeline across Sales, SE, and Dev Success
Days 31–60

Design and Align

  • Cross-team working group to co-design handoff protocols and shared metrics
  • Define the expansion unit: audit closed-won expansion, interview top 10 accounts
  • Design field-to-product feedback loop and automated health score model
  • Begin shadow metrics. Pilot AI handoff summaries on 5 accounts.
Days 61–90

Activate and Iterate

  • Launch operating rhythm: weekly standups, monthly reviews, first QBR
  • Operationalize STS: assign TAMs to strategic accounts, deploy IS on priority onboarding
  • Activate product feedback loop: first monthly field intelligence report to Product
  • Publish 6-month roadmap from shadow metrics to blended comp to consumption-forward
